# What Actually Happens in a First Session

Most people hover over the "send" button on that first email for days. Sometimes weeks. If that's you right now: hello, you're normal.

Here is what a first session with me actually looks like, so the unknown is a little less unknown.

## Before you arrive

Nothing to prepare. You do not need to have your story organized, your childhood mapped, or a diagnosis in mind. People often apologize for "rambling" in a first session — rambling is fine. Rambling is data.

## The first ten minutes

We handle the unglamorous parts: confidentiality and what it covers, how scheduling works, questions about the office (yes, that's the actual Golden Gate Bridge out the window; no, Boston will not sit on you unless invited).

## The rest of the hour

Mostly, I listen. I'll ask questions like:

- What made now the moment you reached out?
- What would need to be different for you to feel like this was worth it?
- Who and what is holding you up right now?

## What I'm *not* doing

I'm not diagnosing you in the first hour, judging your coping mechanisms, or deciding whether your problems are "bad enough" for therapy. If you're wondering that — they are. Suffering doesn't have a minimum threshold.

At the end, we talk about fit. Not every therapist is right for every person, and a good first session includes the freedom to say "this isn't it." If I think someone else would serve you better, I'll help you find them.
