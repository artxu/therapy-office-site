# Rest Is Not a Reward

Somewhere along the way, most of us absorbed a rule: rest must be earned. Finish the list, answer the emails, be useful — *then* you may stop.

The trouble is the list never finishes. So rest keeps receding, like a horizon.

## Burnout isn't a willpower problem

By the time clients say the word "burnout" in my office, they've usually already tried harder. Harder at working, and then — this is the strange part — harder at resting. Optimized sleep, scheduled mindfulness, vacations planned like military operations. Rest becomes another performance to fail at.

> You are a person, not a production line. Depletion is information, not weakness.

## The redwoods don't earn winter

I hike in the redwoods most weekends, and I've never once seen a tree justify its dormant season. Growth in living systems is cyclical — expansion, then consolidation. Every wisdom tradition figured this out millennia before productivity culture forgot it. The Dao De Jing puts it simply: the usefulness of a bowl is in its emptiness.

## Small, unearned rest

Start embarrassingly small:

- Ten minutes sitting somewhere without your phone, before anything is finished.
- One evening a week that has no goal attached to it.
- Saying "let me get back to you" instead of yes.

Notice what comes up when you try — the guilt, the itch, the voice saying you're falling behind. That voice is where the real work is. It has a history, and it can be renegotiated.
