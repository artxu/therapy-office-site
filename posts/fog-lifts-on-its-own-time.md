# The Fog Lifts on Its Own Time

If you live in San Francisco long enough, you stop fighting the fog. You learn its rhythms — how it pours over Twin Peaks in the evening, how it burns off the bay by late morning, how some days it simply stays.

I think about this often when clients tell me they want to *get rid of* their anxiety.

> We heal not by forcing change, but by making room for it.

Anxiety, like fog, is weather. It has causes, patterns, and — importantly — it moves. The instinct to wrestle it into submission usually tightens its grip: now you're anxious *and* frustrated that you're anxious.

## What helps instead

- **Naming the weather.** "There's a lot of fog this morning" is kinder and more accurate than "I'm broken."
- **Warm layers.** Routines, sleep, movement, people who love you — the things that make bad weather survivable.
- **Patience without passivity.** You don't control when fog lifts, but you can choose not to build your whole day around pretending it isn't there.

This is the heart of what Daoist philosophy calls *wu wei* — often mistranslated as "doing nothing," it's closer to *not forcing*. The fog lifts. It always has. Our work, together, is to make the waiting livable and to notice the patterns so the weather surprises you less.
